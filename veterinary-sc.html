Course Overview

Veterinary sciences programs prepare students to diagnose, treat, and prevent animal diseases across companion, farm, and wildlife contexts. Training includes anatomy, pathology, pharmacology, surgery, nutrition, and herd-health management. Practical farm postings and clinical internships are central to professional readiness.

Key Topics Covered

  • Veterinary Anatomy and Physiology
  • Animal Nutrition and Livestock Management
  • Veterinary Pathology and Microbiology
  • Parasitology and Preventive Veterinary Medicine
  • Veterinary Pharmacology and Toxicology
  • Animal Reproduction and Gynecology
  • Veterinary Surgery and Radiology Basics
  • Clinical Medicine for Small and Large Animals
  • Herd Health and Epidemiology
  • Field Training and Internship Practice
