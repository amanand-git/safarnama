Course Overview

Bachelor of Laws (LLB) is a professional degree in legal studies that prepares students for careers in law and justice system. The 3-year or 5-year program covers constitutional law, criminal law, civil law, corporate law, and procedural law. Graduates can practice as advocates, appear for judicial exams, or pursue specialized legal careers in various sectors.
