Course Overview

BDS is the primary undergraduate degree for becoming a dental professional in India. The course combines foundational medical sciences with specialized dental subjects such as oral surgery, prosthodontics, orthodontics, periodontics, and community dentistry. Clinical exposure and internship training are key components, helping students develop practical diagnostic and treatment skills for real patients.

Key Topics Covered

  • General Human Anatomy and Dental Anatomy
  • Physiology, Biochemistry, and Oral Histology
  • Dental Materials and Pre-Clinical Prosthodontics
  • Oral Pathology and Microbiology
  • Conservative Dentistry and Endodontics
  • Periodontics and Implant Basics
  • Orthodontics and Dentofacial Development
  • Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery Fundamentals
  • Pedodontics and Preventive Dentistry
  • Public Health Dentistry and Ethics

Key Facts

  • Duration: 5 years total
  • Internship: 1 year compulsory
  • Eligibility: 10+2 with PCB
  • Admission: NEET-UG based
  • Regulation: Dental education standards

Prerequisites

  • Strong PCB fundamentals
  • Fine motor skill readiness
  • Patient communication skills
  • Interest in oral healthcare
  • Consistency for clinical practice

Skills Developed

  • Oral diagnosis and treatment planning
  • Dental procedure assistance and execution
  • Infection control and sterilization
  • Patient counseling and follow-up care
  • Clinical record and case management
