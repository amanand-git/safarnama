Course Overview

LLM is an advanced law program pursued after LLB for specialization and deeper legal research. Programs include coursework, seminars, dissertations, and case analysis in focused domains. The degree supports higher judiciary preparation, legal academia, policy roles, and senior legal practice.

Key Topics Covered

  • Advanced Constitutional Law
  • Jurisprudence and Legal Theory
  • International and Comparative Law
  • Corporate and Commercial Law
  • Criminal Justice and Procedure
  • Intellectual Property Law
  • Human Rights and Public Policy
  • Research Methodology in Law
  • Dissertation and Legal Writing
  • Emerging Areas: Tech and Data Law

Career Opportunities

  • Legal Research Associate
  • Corporate Legal Consultant
  • Policy and Regulatory Analyst
  • Legal Academic or Lecturer
  • Law Firm Associate (specialized)
  • In-house Counsel Support Roles
  • Human Rights and NGO Legal Roles
  • Arbitration and Mediation Practice
  • Judicial Services Preparation
  • PhD in Law Pathway
